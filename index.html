üìù Lesson 1: The Two Writing Systems

Konju has TWO ways to write, just like Japanese has Hiragana/Katakana and Romaji!

System 1: Konju Script (Primary)

  • 26 unique geometric characters
  • Beautiful, artistic, cultural identity
  • Used for formal writing and aesthetics
  • Takes time to master but looks amazing!

System 2: Konju Latin (Secondary)

  • Uses regular English letters (a-z)
  • Easy to type on any keyboard
  • Perfect for learning and digital communication
  • Writes the SAME words, just different characters

üîê Lesson 2: The Cipher System (MOST IMPORTANT!)

This is the KEY to Konju! Instead of memorizing thousands of words, you just need to learn ONE simple rule:

The Golden Rule: +5 Letter Shift

Take any English word ‚Üí Shift each letter forward by 5 positions ‚Üí That's the Konju word!

Step-by-Step Instructions:

Step 1: Write your English word

Example: CAT

Step 2: Shift each letter forward by 5

  • C ‚Üí H (C+5 = H)
  • A ‚Üí F (A+5 = F)
  • T ‚Üí Y (T+5 = Y)

Step 3: Write the result

CAT in Konju = HFY

Step 4: Use Konju Script or Konju Latin

  • Konju Latin: HFY (type with regular keyboard)
  • Konju Script: (write with geometric characters)

Special Rules:

‚ö†Ô∏è Rule 1: Wraparound at the end

When you go past Z, wrap back to A:

  • V ‚Üí A (V+5: V‚ÜíW‚ÜíX‚ÜíY‚ÜíZ‚ÜíA)
  • W ‚Üí B (W+5: W‚ÜíX‚ÜíY‚ÜíZ‚ÜíA‚ÜíB)
  • X ‚Üí C
  • Y ‚Üí D
  • Z ‚Üí E

‚ö†Ô∏è Rule 2: Q becomes KU

Since Konju doesn't have Q or W, when you land on Q during the shift, replace it with KU

Example: HELLO

  • H ‚Üí M
  • E ‚Üí J
  • L ‚Üí Q ‚Üí KU
  • L ‚Üí Q ‚Üí KU
  • O ‚Üí T

HELLO = MJKUKUT

üìê Lesson 3: Basic Grammar & Word Order

The SOV Pattern

Konju uses SOV (Subject-Object-Verb) word order, similar to Japanese and Korean.

English (SVO) Konju (SOV)
I eat food I food eat
She sees the cat She the cat sees
We speak Konju We Konju speak
üí° Memory Trick: In Konju, the verb always comes last!

üîß Lesson 4: Particles (The Building Blocks)

Particles are small words that attach to nouns and verbs to show their grammatical function. They're ESSENTIAL in Konju!

The 7 Essential Particles

Particle Function English Equivalent Example
-xeu Subject marker "[subject]" (the doer) N-xeu = "I" (as subject)
-y Object marker "[object]" (receiving action) HFY-y = "cat" (as object)
-o Possessive "'s" or "of" N-o = "my"
-a Location "at, in, on" MTZHJ-a = "at home"
-eu Past tense "ed" (past) JFY-eu = "ate"
-yae Future tense "will" JFY-yae = "will eat"
-i Plural "s" (plural) HFY-i = "cats"
-ju Progressive/Continuous "-ing" (doing now) JFY-ju = "eating"

üìå How Particles Attach:

Particles are added directly to the end of words with a hyphen:

  • HFY (cat) + -xeu (subject) = HFY-xeu (the cat [as subject])
  • KTTI (food) + -y (object) = KTTI-y (food [as object])
  • JFY (eat) + -eu (past) = JFY-eu (ate)

Particle Order When Combining

For Nouns: Plural ‚Üí Case Marker

cats (as subject) = HFY-i-xeu

dogs (as object) = ITL-i-y

For Verbs: Progressive/Tense comes last

was eating = JFY-ju-eu (eating + past)

will be eating = JFY-ju-yae (eating + future)

‚è∞ Lesson 5: Verb Forms & Tenses

Basic Tense Formation

Tense How to Form Example (EAT) Translation
Present Base form (no marker) JFY eat / eats
Past Base + -eu JFY-eu ate
Future Base + -yae JFY-yae will eat
Progressive (Present) Base + -ju JFY-ju eating / is eating
Past Progressive Base + -ju-eu JFY-ju-eu was eating
Future Progressive Base + -ju-yae JFY-ju-yae will be eating

All Forms of "GO" (LT)

  • LT = go / goes
  • LT-eu = went
  • LT-yae = will go
  • LT-ju = going / is going
  • LT-ju-eu = was going
  • LT-ju-yae = will be going

Prefix Meaning Example Translation

Suffixes (Add to the end)

Suffix Meaning Example Translation

Building Complex Words

Example: From "SPEAK" (XUJFP) to Related Words

  • XUJFP = speak (verb)
  • XUJFP-ka = speaker, orator (person who speaks)
  • XUJFP-lo = microphone, speech tool (thing for speaking)
  • XUJFP-su = auditorium, speaking place
  • XUJFP-zi = spoken, verbal (adjective)
  • re-XUJFP = speak again, repeat
  • fe-XUJFP = not speak, silent

Compound Words (Combining Roots)

Create new words by combining two root words together:

Pattern: Word1 + Word2 = New Meaning

  • BFYJW (water) + MTZHJ (home) = BFYJWMTZHJ (bathroom)
  • XJJ (see) + -lo (thing) = XJJLO (mirror, seeing-thing)
  • GNL (big) + HFY (cat) = GNLHFY (big cat / lion)
  • XRFKUKU (small) + ITL (dog) = XRFKUKUITL (puppy)

üí¨ Lesson 7: Building Sentences

The Complete Sentence Formula

Subject-xeu + Object-y + Verb(-tense)

Step-by-Step Sentence Construction

Example: "I eat food"

Step 1: Convert each word to Konju

  • I ‚Üí N
  • EAT ‚Üí JFY
  • FOOD ‚Üí KTTI

Step 2: Rearrange to SOV order

English: I eat food

Konju order: I food eat

Step 3: Add particles

  • N + -xeu (subject marker) = N-xeu
  • KTTI + -y (object marker) = KTTI-y
  • JFY (no particle needed for present tense verb)

Final Sentence: N-xeu KTTI-y JFY

Translation: "I eat food"

More Complex Sentences

"The cat was eating fish at home"

Words:

  • CAT ‚Üí HFY
  • EAT ‚Üí JFY
  • FISH ‚Üí KNXM
  • HOME ‚Üí MTZHJ

Structure:

cat-SUBJECT home-LOCATION fish-OBJECT eating-PAST

Konju: HFY-xeu MTZHJ-a KNXM-y JFY-ju-eu

Breakdown:

  • HFY-xeu = the cat (subject)
  • MTZHJ-a = at home (location)
  • KNXM-y = fish (object)
  • JFY-ju-eu = was eating (progressive + past)

"My dogs will go to the park"

Words:

  • I ‚Üí N
  • DOG ‚Üí ITL
  • GO ‚Üí LT
  • PARK ‚Üí UFWP

Structure:

my dogs-PLURAL-SUBJECT park-LOCATION go-FUTURE

Konju: N-o ITL-i-xeu UFWP-a LT-yae

Breakdown:

  • N-o = my (possessive)
  • ITL-i-xeu = dogs (plural + subject)
  • UFWP-a = to/at the park (location)
  • LT-yae = will go (future)

Questions in Konju

To make a question, simply change the word order by moving the verb to the beginning:

Statement Question
N-xeu KTTI-y JFY
(I food eat)		 JFY N-xeu KTTI-y?
(Eat I food? = Do I eat food?)
HFY-xeu BFYJW-y XJJ
(Cat water sees)		 XJJ HFY-xeu BFYJW-y?
(See cat water? = Does cat see water?)

Negatives in Konju

Use the prefix fe- on the verb to make it negative:

  • N-xeu KTTI-y JFY = I eat food
  • N-xeu KTTI-y fe-JFY = I don't eat food

üìñ Quick Reference Guide

üéØ Core Grammar Pattern

Subject-xeu + (Location-a) + Object-y + Verb(-tense)

üìå Essential Particles Cheat Sheet

  • -xeu = subject marker ("the doer")
  • -y = object marker ("receiver of action")
  • -o = possessive ("'s" or "of")
  • -a = location ("at, in, on")
  • -eu = past tense ("-ed")
  • -yae = future tense ("will")
  • -ju = progressive ("-ing", doing now)
  • -i = plural ("-s")

üîß Word Building Tools

Prefixes:

  • fe- = opposite, not (fe-LTTI = bad)
  • re- = again (re-LT = return)

Suffixes:

  • -ka = person (JFY-ka = eater)
  • -lo = thing (XJJ-lo = mirror)
  • -su = place (JFY-su = restaurant)
  • -zi = adjective (JFY-zi = edible)
